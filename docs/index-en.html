Abstract

RSM topology provides fundamental railway network description, focused on connections and navigabilities between net elements. Net elements can be linear (embodied by lines or tracks) or non linear (embodied by stations, yards, or wider network areas). Geometry aspects are delegated to GeoSPARQL and are not essential to topology. Functional aspects (such as "current switch setting") also are not part of the topology.

RSM topology: Description back to ToC

Full RSM topology. Connexities and Navigabilities as object properties . Additionally, navigability reification is included. Temporal aspects to be covered by annotations and/or named graphs and/or dul:Situations, or similar. Syntactic sugar: some properties that are most likely to be used are chained properties, equivalent to derived associations in UML.

connected withop back to ToC or Object Property ToC

IRI: https://cdm.ovh/rsm/topology/topo#connectedWith

Relation between a port on a net element and an adjacent port on another net element. This is a symmetric relation. Geographic locations of related ports may not coincide exactly, either because of constructive tolerances (abutted tracks...), or uncertainties, or rounding errors, or by construction (e.g. case of tracks joined via a turntable).

has characteristics: symmetric

First port of some net element. Allows to orient linear elements in space, even though linear elements may be completely symmetrical from a functional point of view.
